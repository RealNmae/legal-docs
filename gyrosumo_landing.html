GYROSUMO

A shared experiment in physical presence

What does your body communicate when words aren't involved? GyroSumo uses sensors and shared space to find out — together.

"Socializing is a black box. This project replaces social performance. We use accelerometer sensors and AR spatial tracking to explore the raw human reality that exists beneath our social firewalls."

🤫

Mode 01

Still Sync

Everyone holds their phone still. The accelerometer watches. Every micro-tremor is visible to the group — a live portrait of your nervous system, right now, in this room.

  • Real-time motion graph shared across all devices
  • Three sensitivity levels: Casual · Normal · Strict
  • Gentle haptic & visual feedback on movement
  • Up to 8 people on the same WiFi network
  • Calibration mode to explore your baseline first
🌀

Mode 02

King of the Hill

Each person plants an AR zone in the room's actual floor space. Where you choose to stand — and how you negotiate that — becomes the whole conversation.

  • ARKit anchors zones to your real physical space
  • Proximity scoring — presence matters more than speed
  • Shake interrupts your own score — stillness is rewarded
  • Live positions of everyone synced across all devices
  • Adjustable zone sizes & sensitivity for every group

How it works

Same room,
same network, begin.

1

Gather in the same space

Everyone opens GyroSumo on the same WiFi network. The app finds each other automatically — no accounts, no friend lists, no setup.

2

Choose how to explore

Still Sync asks everyone to hold perfectly still together — tension made visible. King of the Hill anchors each person's zone to the physical room and lets presence do the talking.

3

For King of the Hill: point at the floor

Everyone points at the same spot on the floor for a moment. ARKit aligns coordinates across devices — your shared space is now mapped.

4

See what happens

The group moves, holds, negotiates. Your sensors report back honestly. What emerges is different every time — and usually surprising.

Social Experiment

What do we say to each other
through our bodies?

Most social interaction is performance — managed, filtered, curated. GyroSumo strips that away. The sensors don't know how to lie. What's left is something more honest: proximity, stillness, tremor, presence. A conversation without words.
